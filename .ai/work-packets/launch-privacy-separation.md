# Work Packet: launch-privacy-separation

Status: completed
Owner: orchestrator
Source: User privacy requirement before public launch
Branch: launch/production

## Intent

Separate exact voter identity/location data from AI chat so Voter Choice cannot combine "who said what" with "where they live" in app-controlled records, and make privacy tradeoffs visible at the moment a user uses each sensitive feature.

## Original User Intent

Protect people as much as possible, avoid any path where the app can reveal who said what and where they live, do not send exact address to Anthropic, warn users not to share identifying details in chat, and add small inline privacy explanations.

## Intent Interpretation

Google Civic may receive exact address to resolve official ballot/polling data. Anthropic should receive only coarse ballot context and contests, never the exact address or polling-place street addresses supplied by Google. Users should see concise in-context privacy warnings without needing to read the full privacy policy.

## Business Logic

Rules:

- Exact address may be used for Google Civic/Places lookup and local UI display.
- Exact address must not be included in Anthropic prompts generated by the app.
- Polling-place street addresses must not be included in Anthropic prompts generated by the app.
- Chat UI must warn users not to type name, exact address, phone, email, or other identifying details.
- Profile/output copy must not claim no external transmission when chat/profile context is sent to Anthropic.

Assumptions:

- ZIP and county are acceptable coarse location for AI ballot research.
- Polling-place names and counts are less sensitive than street addresses, but the safest launch path omits polling places from AI prompt context entirely.

User-confirmed decisions:

- Implement all privacy-separation changes before launch.

Out of scope:

- Enterprise zero-data-retention contracts with Anthropic.
- Legal review or formal subpoena-resistance certification.
- Removing Google Civic/Places address lookup.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Generated prompts do not contain exact user street address.
- Generated prompts do not contain polling-place or early-vote street addresses.
- Prompt tells AI not to request identifying details.
- Chat UI warns users not to type identifying details.
- Address UI explains address goes to Google, not AI, and is not stored by us.
- Profile upload/output copy accurately says profile goes to Anthropic if used in chat.
- Privacy policy explains the Google/Anthropic separation.
